## 1. Rates & CB Policy

### Overview

BLOCK OVERVIEW

Purpose:

This block tracks the Federal Reserve's interest rate decisions, forward guidance, balance sheet operations, yield curve shape, and real interest rates. It captures the monetary policy environment — the single most powerful macro driver of equity valuations, credit conditions, and liquidity flows.

Why It Matters:

Central bank policy sets the cost of capital for the entire economy. When rates are low and falling, risk assets benefit from cheaper borrowing, lower discount rates, and abundant liquidity. When rates are high and rising, valuations compress, credit tightens, and recession risk increases. The yield curve and QE/QT stance amplify or dampen these effects.

Interpretation:

A high block score (4–5) = accommodative environment, supportive for equities. A low score (1–2) = restrictive policy, headwind for risk assets. A score of 3 = neutral/transitional stance.

## 2. Inflation & Labor

### Overview

BLOCK OVERVIEW

Purpose:

This block monitors consumer price inflation (headline and core CPI), labor market health (unemployment, payrolls, participation), and wage dynamics. These are the key inputs the Fed watches to calibrate monetary policy — they determine whether the Fed tightens, holds, or eases.

Why It Matters:

Inflation erodes purchasing power and forces the Fed to raise rates, compressing equity multiples. A strong labor market supports consumer spending and corporate earnings, but overheating wages can trigger more tightening. The ideal environment for equities is moderate inflation (2–3%), low unemployment (3.5–4.5%), and wage growth that tracks productivity (~3–4%).

Interpretation:

A high block score (4–5) = Goldilocks: inflation cooling toward target with a healthy labor market. A low score (1–2) = stagflation risk (high inflation + weakening jobs) or overheating (inflation surging, forcing aggressive Fed response). Score of 3 = mixed signals, data-dependent.

## 3. Sentiment & Risk

### Overview

BLOCK OVERVIEW

Purpose:

This block measures market psychology, fear/greed dynamics, options positioning, implied volatility, and market breadth. These are real-time behavioral indicators that capture how investors are FEELING and POSITIONING — often leading price moves by days or weeks.

Why It Matters:

Markets are driven by human emotion as much as fundamentals. Extreme fear creates contrarian buying opportunities ("buy when others are fearful"). Extreme greed signals complacency and vulnerability to correction. The VIX, put/call ratios, and breadth indicators provide quantitative measures of crowd psychology that can signal turning points before they appear in price.

Interpretation:

IMPORTANT — This block uses CONTRARIAN logic for some indicators. Extreme fear (low raw scores) can actually be BULLISH from a contrarian standpoint. The scoring below already incorporates this: Score 5 = conditions most favorable for future equity returns (which may mean extreme fear = buying opportunity).

## 5. Business Cycle

### Overview

BLOCK OVERVIEW

Purpose:

This block positions the economy within the business cycle using manufacturing surveys (ISM PMI), composite leading indicators (LEI), style rotation signals (small vs large cap, growth vs value), credit market stress (high yield spreads), and equity issuance activity (IPOs). It answers: Are we in early cycle recovery, mid-cycle expansion, late-cycle overheating, or recession?

Why It Matters:

The business cycle determines which asset classes, sectors, and styles outperform. Early cycle favors small caps, cyclicals, and high-beta. Mid-cycle favors quality growth. Late cycle rewards defensives and value. Recession demands cash and bonds. Credit spreads are the market's real-time pricing of default risk — widening spreads have preceded every recession and equity bear market. The LEI is the most reliable composite forward indicator.

Interpretation:

A high block score (4–5) = early-to-mid cycle expansion with healthy credit and rising risk appetite. A low score (1–2) = late cycle or recession with widening spreads, falling LEI, and defensive rotation. Score of 3 = mid-cycle maturity or transition.
